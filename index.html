How Tourism Drives Economic Growth and Recovery in Malaysia

Tourism is a key engine for Malaysia’s economy, shaping employment, spending, and growth across regions and sectors. From 2015 to 2024, the data reveals how tourist arrivals, industry jobs, and expenditure patterns have responded to global challenges and local strengths. Despite setbacks during the pandemic, Malaysia’s tourism sector has shown resilience, with strong rebounds in arrivals, employment, and spending. These visualisations highlight the dynamic links between tourism and economic development, offering insights into recovery and future opportunities.

Arrivals by State (selectable year)

Description

Tourist arrivals in Malaysia show clear regional leaders, with Selangor, Kuala Lumpur, and Johor consistently attracting the most visitors. The interactive map highlights these trends and shows strong recovery in 2024, reflecting Malaysia's enduring appeal across its states.

Tourism Employment by Industry Sector (2015-2024)

Description

Tourism employment in Malaysia is driven by food, retail, and accommodation sectors. Despite a dip in 2020-2021, jobs rebounded, reaching new highs in 2024.

Inbound Tourism Expenditure of Excursionist by Products

Description

Inbound tourism spending in Malaysia dropped sharply in 2020-2021, but has since recovered across all product categories, with accommodation and transport leading the rebound.

GDP / GVA Growth & International Arrivals

Description

GDP and GVA growth rates peaked in 2017, alongside the highest international arrivals. The chart shows how tourism growth and visitor numbers move together over time.

Average Length of Stay by Country (2015-2017)

Description

Visitors from different countries show varying length of stays in Malaysia. European and Middle Eastern countries tend to have longer stays, while neighboring ASEAN countries show shorter average stays.
